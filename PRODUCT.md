# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The dashboard serves a broad audience of active traders, derivatives researchers, and institutionally minded market participants who need to monitor the HYPE options market with speed and confidence. Users are reading the surface during live market work: scanning volatility, open interest, gamma exposure, skew, volume, and expiry structure to decide what deserves deeper analysis.

## Product Purpose

HYPE Data System provides a professional options data panel for HYPE. It turns recurring Derive market snapshots into a dense, real-time dashboard that surfaces spot price, snapshot freshness, option OI and volume, put/call activity, GEX, ATM IV term structure, IV smile, 25D skew/fly, and expiry/strike breakdowns.

Success means a trader can quickly understand the current options regime, identify where positioning and volatility are concentrated, and trust that units, timestamps, chart controls, and stale-data states are explicit.

## Brand Personality

Professional, precise, institutional.

The interface should feel like a serious trading terminal: black surface, high information density, clear hierarchy, restrained color, and technically sharp typography. It should borrow the smoothness and component discipline of Velo-like market tooling without becoming flashy, decorative, or consumer-fintech styled.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboard card grids, marketing-page composition, oversized hero sections, low information density, over-decorated UI, ornamental gradients, glass effects, and arbitrary colorful accents.

Avoid visuals that make the dashboard feel like a demo template instead of a live trading tool. Avoid ambiguity in units, timestamps, metric availability, and data freshness. Avoid presenting unavailable or statistically weak metrics as if they were complete.

## Design Principles

1. Data clarity first: every metric needs clear units, formatting, freshness, and enough context to be interpreted correctly.
2. Terminal discipline: dense information is acceptable when structure, alignment, and chart controls stay predictable.
3. Institutional restraint: color communicates data roles and state, not decoration.
4. Local control: chart-level expiry, side, and window controls belong close to the chart they affect.
5. Trust through state: live, stale, loading, missing, and insufficient-history states must be visible and honest.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support reduced motion preferences. Do not rely on red/green alone to communicate meaning; pair color with labels, signs, position, or legend text where practical. Keep text legible at dashboard density and prevent labels, controls, or chart annotations from overlapping at common desktop and mobile breakpoints.
